Why Hawaii?

"Travelling to Hawaii provides an ability to experience different culture and love for nature" 

Hawaii islands has around 137 islands with 4 majors ones. Those are the Hawaii Island (Big Island), Maui, Oahu, Kanai, Lanai, Molokai, Niihau, and Kahoolawe.

Geography: It has around 6400 sq. Miles and 1.4 million people living in it. Out of which almost 2/3 of the population reside in Oahu island. 

Hawaii was first settled by Polynesians between 300-600AD. It then became a US. Territory in 1900 and 50th US State in August 21st 1959. The state capital is Honolulu which is located in Oahu Island.

Entry Requirements: As the Hawaii is a state of USA, entry requirement is same as mainland of USA. Pre- approved US Visa is required to visit Hawaii, except citizen of 40 countries who are eligible for Visa Waiver Program. These countries include Canada, United Kingdom, all major European countries, Australia, New Zealand. Except Canada all citizen of countries listed in Visa Waiver Program must have a valid "Electronic System of Travel Authorization (ESTA)" prior to board any US bound carrier. ESTA remain valid for two years or expiry date of passport whichever occurred first. For Canadian citizen except passport no other document is required to enter USA for six months.

Transportation: Since, Hawaii are comprises of islands, only method to arrive here by air only. Airlines from various parts of world as well as many states of USA are landed in various islands of Hawaii. Best way of travelling inside an island is rent a car or book a conducted tour, Avail public transportation is not a good option and it is not available at all islands.

It allows visitors to appreciate the beauty, culture, and the activities that the island life offers. 

  1. CULTURE: The culture of Hawaii is about 1500 years and filled with history. Along with history, it’s filled fascinating traditions, values, legends, and customs. The culture has its origins from the Polynesians and based around traditional gods and goddesses. It includes: Pele (a wicked volcano goddess), Poliahu (a snow god), and Maui (a demi-god).          Hula: Hula signifies one of Hawaii’s key cultural icon. It includes people in      colourful       costumes and grass skirts, who dance with graceful hand movements  and its       signature swaying hips,                                                                                                         Religion: The traditional Hawaiian religion is polytheistic animistic religion where      Hawaiians believe there are spirits. They believe in 4 main gods: Kāne (God of          creation), Kanaloa (God of ocean), Ku (God of war), and Lono (God of peace). They also        believe in ka hā (40 male gods), ke kanahā (400 gods and goddesses), na ‘unihipili      (spirits) and na ‘aumākua (guardians).                                                                                                 Music: It is an important part of culture and has a unique mix of many influences that        celebrate the island life. The key symbols of Hawaii’s musical culture are ukulele,       and reggae to slack-key and steel guitar, falsetto and “Jawaiian”.

ACTIVITY Whether interested in surfing, or simply sightseeing, there is an activity for everyone.                                                                                                                                                  Sightseeing - Sightseeing: The beautiful mountain ranges, deep blue oceans, diverse      landscapes makes it a beautiful place to sight see in the Hawaii.                       Helicopter Tours - To see the natural beautiful at a glance, the helicopter tours allows     to see Hawaii with a bird’s eye view.                                                                                     Snorkelling - Snorkelling allows visitors to see and learn about the diverse and      colourful marine life that exists under the ocean.                                                             Luau- Luau is Hawaiian and Polynesian style luau feasts. It allows visitors to learn     about Hawaiian arts like basket weaving and also partake in the culture. Cutural     presentations like Hawaiian hula dances and fire dance performances immerses people     in its history and culture.                                                                                                                Surfing - The Islands of Hawaii still remain on the forefront as the best place in the     world for surfing. Visitors travel to Hawaii from all corners of the world for surfing.     Surfing is prevalent on all the Hawaiian Islands,                                                                                                                                
  3. ENVIRONMENT  - Whether it's beach, forest or mountains, the beauty can be all round.     Hawaii's beautiful mountains and beaches make it one of the most famous travel destinations.                                                                                                                                                    Climate: In Hawaiian islands, the weather is diverse but tropical climate is most     common. This allows visitors to enjoy the warm weather and its beaches. For rainfall,     Hawaii receives its most precipitation from October to April. From May to September,     it’s much drier weather. Temperatures can also differ for the islands The sea level     varies from 26 to 31C, but higher up the temperature lowers. It can even have snow in     summits of Mauna Kea, Mauna Loa during winter time.
